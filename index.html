Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

(1931 - 2015)

Biography

APJ Abdul Kalam, full name Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was an eminent Indian scientist and statesman. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, and passed away on July 27, 2015. Dr. Kalam is widely regarded as the "Missile Man of India" for his significant contributions to India's space and missile programs. After completing his education in aerospace engineering, Kalam joined the Defense Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He played a crucial role in the development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle and ballistic missile systems. In 2002, APJ Abdul Kalam became the 11th President of India, serving a five-year term. During his presidency, he continued to inspire the nation through his vision for a developed India and his dedication to youth empowerment and education. Dr. Kalam was known for his humility, simplicity, and strong work ethic, which endeared him to people of all ages and backgrounds. Even after leaving the presidency, Kalam remained an influential figure, promoting education and scientific research. He authored several books, including the famous autobiography "Wings of Fire," which narrates his life journey and experiences. APJ Abdul Kalam's legacy continues to inspire millions of people in India and around the world. He is remembered not only for his immense contributions to science and technology but also for his passion for serving humanity and his relentless pursuit of knowledge and excellence.

His Tremendous Achievements

Making India a nuclear power:

Dr APJ Abdul Kalam headed multiple nuclear test programmes at Pokhran in the early 90s. As the Chief Scientific Adviser to the then Prime Minister, Dr Kalam played a major role in heading the Pokhran-II nuclear tests which made India a nuclear power.
